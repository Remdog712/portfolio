INSIDE THE VOID - Game Review









Game Description

Inside The Void can be described as an exploration game. You play as a lone operative from an unknown group and are tasked with landing on a planet and piecing together the mystery of what happened to the crew that was there before. You must explore and visit every void to unravel the mystery.

The Setting

Inside The Void has an incredibly vibrant and unique planet for its landscape. It's quite barren but it makes up for it in color. The almost empty area doesn’t distract you from the main quest but certainly keeps you glued into the world. An almost emerald colored ground that expands for what seems like miles plays well into the game’s exploration, as well as voids that are scattered in almost a circle surrounding where you begin the game.





The Characters

There are roughly three characters if you could call them characters. There’s the missing crew whom you spend the game finding out what happened to them. There’s a Commander at the very start of the game who tasks you with exploring the planet, and finally there’s Operative 1286 and that’s the player character. You never see the player character and I think that plays well into not worrying about anything other than exploring, learning, and completing the game. There are no distractions that lead you astray.





The Objects

There are essentially four types of objects in the game. There’s voids, cores, entry logs, and creatures. The creatures are nearly non-existent. They are mentioned several times and pop up in the backgrounds but never seem to encounter the player, but adding enemies or a threat of the sort would take away from the core gameplay and I find it to be a very smart choice by 3D Methods. The next kinds of objects are the voids and cores. They go hand-in-hand. The voids are scattered across the landscape and are each very unique in shape so as to not feel like you visited somewhere twice. They teleport you inside as you get close with a very unique wavy animation as the screen fades. The cores are found within the voids and represent the voids that they are within. Each core has its own animation before they “warble/teleport” you out. The final types of objects found are the entry logs. This is essentially what gives the game a story. The entry logs were left by a previous crew and give small tidbits of information on what happened on the planet and why the crew is stranded. They are all found in different areas and are numbered to ensure that you understand the story and haven’t missed anything.

The Meaning

The meaning of the game could be interpreted in many ways. There isn’t a definitive answer which I believe makes a game very interesting. The ending makes you think for a moment of what it all means. I find that the meaning of the game is quite “meta.” That the player character is a representation of you going into the game to help this crew escape from the game. The planet is laid out in words as essentially a giant computer with simulated universes and data inside. SPOILER ALERT. Near the end of the game, you encounter a large tower that the missing crew describe as a computer with many processing components to host the mini-civilizations that live on the planet. I believe that the crew is unaware that they’re in a computer, your computer and you must go inside and save them.

Game Review

I believe this game needs little to no changes. There are no enemies, there are no challenges, it’s a very lonely game but I believe that some games work solely as they stand and this is one of them. No need for AAA graphics when it’s the story that sits in the spotlight. The mystery left even after the credits roll is an ingenious method to keep the player thinking about the game and possibly playing it again to try and understand. I very much enjoyed the game and hope for more games to come from 3D Methods.
